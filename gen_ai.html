WHAT IS GENERATIVE AI?

Throughout this project, we have referred to generative AI or gen AI countless times. But what actually is it? Since our research involved the use of ChatGPT, this is going to be the generative AI software we zero in on. ChatGPT was created by a company called OpenAI, and it is essentially a giant neural network (Gregersen). A neural network is an algorithm that is structured in a way similar to the human brain.

Essentially, ChatGPT is a software that was built modeling off aspects of a human, and it performs similarly to a human (Wolfram). ChatGPT continuously produces its outputs by continuing to predict one word after another. The order of the words it outputs is predicted based on probabilities (what is the probability that the correct word should be this word) (Wolfram). Because ChatGPT produces its outputs entirely based on probabilities, the outputs it produces are not always accurate. ChatGPT even includes a disclaimer that explains that the software is not 100% trustworthy.

ChatGPT is able to function because it is “trained” on data. Training a model on data means that a developer introduces the model to a bunch of different data (in ChatGPT’s case, many different websites). This data allows the model to “learn” and, therefore, make predictions when it is asked to do a certain task. These predictions then turn into the outputs that the Gen AI models produce. ChatGPT specifically was trained on about 45 terabytes of text from the internet (Gregersen). For context, that is a lot of data!
